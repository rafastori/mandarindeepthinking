A: It's the dreaded math class again. Agh, Wang Xiaoming, after we get out of school let's go play soccer.
B: Li Jia, what are you saying? Stand up and explain to us what fractions are.
A: Fractions show how many parts one number is of another.
B: Can you give an example?
A: For example, if I take one apple from three apples, that is one third.
B: Good, then how do we write it?
A: First write the fraction line. "The top" is the numerator, and is written on top of the fraction line. "The bottom" is the denominator, and is written underneath.
B: Good. Then what is percentage?
A: That is a fraction of a hundred, for example: one fourth is twenty-five percent; one half is fifty percent.
B: Then what is "several tenths"?
A: "A tenth" is a fraction of ten. For example, "nine tenths" is nine over ten, and also is ninety percent.
